Mission & Policy Document

Change the Senate, Change Nigeria

Mission Goals

It is the job of the Senate to make legislation that secure, unite and make Nigeria productive. This is a long-term project that requires consistency. We can not achieve much of that with the people who do not care or with those who secured their candidature with a N100m application form.

My mission goal is to change Nigeria and that will require changing those that represent us in the upper chambers of the National Assembly. We cannot do the same thing and get a different result. We cannot elect the same crop of politicians and change the country.

So, I have the honour today to present myself to you as the next senator of Lagos Central Senatorial district – God willing!

Blessing Oladeji

7-Point Legislative Agenda

The elders say “a tree does not make a forest”. As we seek to elect a new President in 2023, you will agree that an effective President needs a competent Senate. We need to constitute a Senate that will bring the people hope and instill confidence in investors who will facilitate our economic recovery.

It is in our hands to determine the kind of society we want to live in, how we want to ensure common welfare and those who should represent us to achieve these common aspirations. One mistake we should not make is to elect people who are not deeply connected with our pains in Lagos Central or representatives that will not put the constituents first. We should not vote for those who make us feel more and more helpless about the change we desire.

In this era of economic instability, hyper inflation, security challenges and incessant ASUU strike, if we want to change the narrative, then, we must change the Senate!

Agenda 1: Stimulate Growth Through Youth Employment and Job Creation

Nigeria’s youth unemployment rate is 42.5%. This implies that more than 4 out of every 10 Nigerian youths that can work and available to work can not find work. More so, Lagos also ranked the worst states with the rate of unemployment having about 2 million people without jobs according to a 2020 report by Statisence.

My agenda if elected is to promote sustained and inclusive economic growth with full and productive employment for Lagosians through the following:

  1. Legislate development-oriented policies that support productive activities, job creation, entrepreneurship, creativity and innovation,
  2. Encourage the formalization and growth of micro-, small- and medium-sized enterprises, including through access to financial services
  3. Develop and operationalize strategy for youth employment and organize mentoring programs that will help young people better prepare for their future jobs.
  4. Ensure that Lagos State government improve resource efficiency in consumption, government contracts and production.

Agenda 2: Fast-tracking Lekki Regional Road & Flood Control

According to Economic Intelligence Unit, our Lagos, with a relatively score of 32.2%, was just a little above Syria’s war-torn capital, Damascus, and a place behind Libya’s Tripoli as the second-worst city to live worldwide. Eko ti n baje!

Eku iroju! My heart goes out to Lagosians especially the residents of Etiosa from Lekki Phase 1 to Sangotedo. The traffic and the flood on this axis are unbearable. These have to change! And it is now or never!

As a federal legislator, I may not be able to do much with regards to actual execution of these projects but my agenda for Lekki is as follows:

  1. Ensure that the state executives (including the state governor) and its MDAs, remain transparent and responsive to the speedy completion of the Lekki Regional road.
  2. Ensure the deployment of at least 500 BRT busses and modern carparks on Lekki axis to decongest the city of danfo and private vehicles.
  3. Introduce flood warning systems and strengthen flood control capacity in all 62 wards in Lagos Central.
  4. Improve awareness-raising and human and institutional capacity on climate change mitigation, adaptation, impact reduction and early warning.

Agenda 3: Formulate Policy to Grow the Nigeria Tech-Economy

Data Science, Artificial Intelligence, Cybersecurity, Cryptocurrency, Web3, Metaverse etc. are not the regular conversations on the floor of both chambers of our National Assembly. However, these are the subjects that will shape our future (the youth) as a country. Tech is an enabler and driver for businesses and economic prosperity. If the future matters to you, then we have to change the composition of the National Assembly with candidates that are capable of reshaping our future.

This is my agenda for the development of Nigeria tech-economy

  1. Facilitate universal access to information and communications technology and ensure affordable access to the Internet to all residents of Lagos Central.
  2. Promote venture capital investments and grow tech start-up funding through diaspora remittance
  3. Sponsor the development technology hubs, tech ecosystems, accelerators for talent development across all the geo-political zones in Nigeria
  4. Support domestic technology development, research and innovation by ensuring a conducive policy environment and economic diversification

Agenda 4: Constitutional Reforms and Restructuring

Nigeria’s constitution is a fundamental aspects of our political processes and democratic existence. In business, it is like the article of association. For startup investments, it is like the term sheet. Any constitution, no matter how perfectly written, will break and cause more complicated problems than it was meant to solve if it cannot blend or bend with the times. The times have changed so should the custodians of our constitution and the constitution!

I align completely with my party's policy on restructuring as one of the sustainable solutions to the problem of insecurity in the nation:

  1. Devolution of power from the center to component units
  2. Decongest the exclusive list which bestowa so much power with little responsibility on the Federal Government
  3. Decentralization of the policing system; Support and encourage states and LGA with capacity to embark on State Policing
  4. Policing is local and the idea of centralized policing is aquake and completely inadequate to address the hydra-headed security situation of the country

Agenda 5: Generate Prosperity through Independent Power Generation

With less than 8000MW power generation and a population of 200m, Nigeria simply does not generate or distribute enough power to meet its needs. Nigeria’s power sector needs far exceed the already stretched public finances. It is crucial for governments to make laws that attract greater levels of private investment to scale up generation capacity and help take electricity to those who need it most. And no other people in Nigeria needs it more than Lagos Central.

My policy on power generation will be as follows:

  1. Promote transparency in the selection of companies to take-over power independent & off-grid generation and distribution across Lagos Central following the passage of bill that allows states generate electricity
  2. Initiate policy that invigorate institutional frameworks for the establishment of a viable and independent state electricity supply agency
  3. Promote and support bills bill would stimulate policy and regulatory measures to scale up efficient power generation, transmission and distribution capabilities of the power sector
  4. Formulate procedures and legislations that address technological limitations and outdated infrastructure that are responsible for value chain loses

Agenda 6: Build More Schools in Lagos Central especially Etiosa

In the 21st century where the world is driven by knowledge, the greatest investment is development of human capital. I believe that education holds the strongest potential in transforming any society. This is why United Nations recommend a sizeable amount of our annual budget towards ensuring that families have access to quality basic education for their kids. In Lagos Central, the situation is different where public schools are inadequate and private schools are very expensive. More than 80% of Lagos Central families send their kids to private schools (from kindergarten to University) because public schools are not great. These have to change. These and much more are my visions for Lagos Central on education:

  1. Build and upgrade education facilities that are child and time sensitive and provide safe, non-violent, inclusive and effective learning environments for all starting with Etiosa
  2. Ensure that all learners acquire the knowledge and skills needed to promote sustainable development within our communities and a culture of peace and non-violence
  3. Substantially facilitate the expansion of the the number of scholarships available to Lagos Central students
  4. Encourage teachers to develop particular transversal competences in themselves on top of the competences specific to their academic subject.

Agenda 7: Promote Innovations to Ease Traffic & Reduce Lagosians Travel Time

The perennial traffic congestions has become a normal phenomenon on our roads such that the people feel helpless and resign to fate. Traffic congestion is not fate. You see people wake up as early as 5am in order to beat the traffic and they do this 5 days a week. These have led to increase in high blood pressures, stress and other health issues among the residents.

We cannnot continue this way. We cannot continue with representatives who do not represent our aspirations. We cannot continue to representatives who cannot challenge incompetency. This have to change.
