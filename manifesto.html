Mission & Policy Document

Change the Senate, Change Nigeria

Mission Goals

It is the job of the Senate to make legislation that secure, unite and make Nigeria productive. This is a long-term project that requires consistency. We can not achieve much of that with the people who do not care or with those who secured their candidature with a N100m application form.

My mission goal is to change Nigeria and that will require changing those that represent us in the upper chambers of the National Assembly. We cannot do the same thing and get a different result. We cannot elect the same crop of politicians and change the country.

So, I have the honour today to present myself to you as the next senator of Lagos Central Senatorial district – God willing!

Blessing Oladeji

7-Point Legislative Agenda

The elders say “a tree does not make a forest”. As we seek to elect a new President in 2023, you will agree that an effective President needs a competent Senate. We need to constitute a Senate that will bring the people hope and instill confidence in investors who will facilitate our economic recovery.

It is in our hands to determine the kind of society we want to live in, how we want to ensure common welfare and those who should represent us to achieve these common aspirations. One mistake we should not make is to elect people who are not deeply connected with our pains in Lagos Central or representatives that will not put the constituents first. We should not vote for those who make us feel more and more helpless about the change we desire.

In this era of economic instability, hyper inflation, security challenges and incessant ASUU strike, if we want to change the narrative, then, we must change the Senate!

Agenda 1: Stimulate Growth Through Youth Employment and Job Creation

Nigeria’s youth unemployment rate is 42.5%. This implies that more than 4 out of every 10 Nigerian youths that can work and available to work can not find work. More so, Lagos also ranked the worst states with the rate of unemployment having about 2 million people without jobs according to a 2020 report by Statisence.

My agenda if elected is to promote sustained and inclusive economic growth with full and productive employment for Lagosians through the following:

  1. Legislate development-oriented policies that support productive activities, job creation, entrepreneurship, creativity and innovation,
  2. Encourage the formalization and growth of micro-, small- and medium-sized enterprises, including through access to financial services
  3. Develop and operationalize strategy for youth employment and organize mentoring programs that will help young people better prepare for their future jobs.
  4. Ensure that Lagos State government improve resource efficiency in consumption, government contracts and production.
